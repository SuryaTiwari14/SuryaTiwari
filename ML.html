ECG & R-Peak Detection

Developing a RedPitayaBased, Compact, and Real-Time Electrocardiogram (ECG) Device for the Accurate Detection of Arrhythmias.

Unveiling the Inner Workings: A Closer Look at the Project's Operations

  • Electrode Placement: The AD8232 sensor is typically used with three electrodes: a positive electrode, a negative electrode, and a reference electrode. These electrodes are placed on the body to pick up the electrical signals produced by the heart
  • Signal Amplification: The sensor amplifies the weak electrical signals from the electrodes to a measurable level. It employs an instrumentation amplifier that provides high common-mode rejection, reducing noise and interference
  • Electrode Impedance Measurement: The AD8232 includes a feature to measure electrode impedance. By injecting a small test current and measuring the voltage drop, it can assess the quality of the electrode contact with the skin. This helps ensure reliable signal acquisition.
  • Output Interface: The AD8232 provides analog output, which represents the amplified and filtered ECG signal. The output can be further processed or displayed using external devices like microcontrollers or data acquisition systems.
  • Filtering and Noise Reduction: The sensor incorporates filters to remove unwanted noise and interference from the ECG signal. This helps ensure a clean and reliable output, free from artifacts that can affect the accuracy of heart rate measurements
  • Power Supply: The AD8232 sensor requires a power supply voltage typically ranging from 2.0V to 3.5V. It can be powered by a battery or a regulated power source, making it suitable for portable and wearable applications.
  • Additional Features: The AD8232 sensor also includes features like lead-off detection, which can identify if the electrode contact is lost or not properly attached. It can provide a signal indicating the presence of artifacts due to poor electrode connection.
