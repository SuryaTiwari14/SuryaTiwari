Internal Mapping System

Using BLE for the connection system and calculating RSSI values. A Three beacons system were built using Arduino nano BLE 33 Sense

Step By Step Explaination

  • Utilize three Arduino Nano BLE 33 Sense devices as beacons.
  • Implement BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy) for the connection system.
  • Measure the RSSI (Received Signal Strength Indicator) values from the three beacons.
  • Apply trilateration method to calculate the device's exact position based on RSSI values.
  • Use the calculated position to map a specific floor within the internal mapping system.
  • Trilateration is a technique used in geometry and navigation to determine the precise location of a point in space by measuring its distance from three known reference points (usually called anchors or beacons). This method relies on the intersection of spheres, where the distances from the point of interest to the reference points are used to calculate its coordinates in a two-dimensional or three-dimensional space. Trilateration is commonly employed in GPS systems, indoor positioning, and various location-based applications to determine the position of objects or devices.
